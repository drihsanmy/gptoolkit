About the Framingham Risk Score

The Framingham Risk Score is a gender‑specific algorithm to estimate the 10‑year cardiovascular disease (CVD) risk. Developed from the Framingham Heart Study, it remains a cornerstone for primary prevention.

Included risk factors:

  • Age & Gender
  • Total & HDL cholesterol
  • Systolic blood pressure & hypertension treatment
  • Smoking status
  • Diabetes mellitus

Risk categories:

  • Low risk: <10% 10‑year CVD risk
  • Intermediate: 10–19.9%
  • High risk: ≥20%

Note: This tool is for educational use; always interpret results alongside clinical judgment.
